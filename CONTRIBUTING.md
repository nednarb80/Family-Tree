# Adding to the family tree

Thank you for helping! Every document, photo, and correction makes this archive
stronger and more accurate. You do **not** need to know anything technical. Pick
whichever way below feels comfortable — they all work.

Everything lives on **GitHub** (a free website for sharing files like this). You'll
need a free GitHub account to contribute; sign up at [github.com](https://github.com).

---

## The three ways to help

### 1. Just tell us (easiest)

Found something but don't want to fuss with files? **Open an "Issue."**

1. Go to the project's GitHub page and click the **Issues** tab.
2. Click **New Issue**.
3. Pick the **"I have a record or correction"** template and fill it in — who it's
   about, what you have, and any details. You can drag a photo right into the box.
4. Submit. The maintainer takes it from there.

That's it. This is perfect for "here's grandma's obituary" or "I think Uncle Ray's
birth year is wrong."

### 2. Upload a document yourself

Have a scan or photo of a real record (a certificate, census page, a photographed
headstone)? You can drop it straight in.

1. On the GitHub page, open the **`_inbox`** folder.
2. Click **Add file → Upload files**.
3. Drag in your image or PDF. Give it a clear name like
   `warner-harry-death-certificate.jpg`.
4. At the bottom, write a one-line note about what it is and click **Propose
   changes**.

The maintainer reviews it, and our document reader turns it into properly cited
updates to the right people.

**What makes a great source:** an *original* record beats a summary every time — a
photo of the actual certificate or census page, not just a typed note saying what it
said. Include where it came from if you know (which archive, which website, a
relative's shoebox).

### 3. Edit a person's page directly (for the adventurous)

If you're comfortable, you can edit the text of a person's page.

1. Navigate to `people/` → the person's folder → their `.md` file.
2. Click the **pencil icon** (Edit).
3. Make your change. The top section (between the `---` lines) holds the facts;
   below it is the life story you can write in plain prose.
4. At the bottom, describe your change and click **Propose changes**. This opens a
   "Pull Request" the maintainer can review and accept.

Don't worry about breaking anything — nothing you propose goes live until it's
reviewed, and every version is saved and reversible.

---

## A few gentle guidelines

- **Cite where you can.** "Birth date from her WWII draft card" is gold. Even
  "family story from Aunt Pat" is worth recording — we just mark how sure we are.
- **Don't overwrite a fact with a guess.** If you think something's wrong, say so in
  a note rather than deleting it — the maintainer will weigh the evidence.
- **Living people:** please be thoughtful about sharing personal details (exact
  addresses, birthdates of living relatives) on a public site.
- **When in doubt, use option 1.** An Issue is never the wrong choice.

Questions? Open an Issue and just ask. Welcome aboard — this is *our* history.
